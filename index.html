Research Experience

Modeling Project: Mathematical Modeling of Leakages in Vacuum Systems

University of Bremen & German Aerospace Center (DLR), Stade

WiSe 2025/26

Developed a physics-based mathematical model and finite element implementation for simulating pressure distributions in vacuum bag systems used in aerospace composite manufacturing at DLR.

Python NumPy Partial Differential Equations Finite Element Method Numerical Simulation Mathematical Modeling
  • Derived weak formulation of Darcy's law for porous media flow and discretized using P1 finite elements.
  • Implemented a complete FEM solver from scratch in Python, including element stiffness matrix assembly and load vector construction.
  • Integrated experimental flow measurements from DLR mass flow controllers into the numerical model as discrete source terms.
  • Verified theoretical properties including matrix symmetry, nullspace structure, and conservation compatibility.

Academic modeling project in collaboration with DLR Stade (Supervision: Dr. Dirk Lorenz, Dr. Christoph Brauer, Dr.-Ing. Arne Hindersmann).
